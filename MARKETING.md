# 90-Day Off-Site Marketing Playbook

_Per spec §14.3: "give the owner a 90-day playbook: GBP optimization, citation building, 5 review-acquisition templates, and 8 blog post titles targeting Atlanta-specific cleaning queries."_

The on-site rebuild gets Red Sky Cleaning to a state where Google can rank the site. **Top-3 in Google Maps for "house cleaning [city]"** also requires off-site work that only the owner (or a designated marketing operator) can do. Below is the 90-day plan, ordered by impact.

## Week 1–2: Google Business Profile (GBP) — single biggest local lever

1. **Claim or verify the GBP** at [business.google.com](https://business.google.com). If already claimed, log in; if not, complete postcard verification.
2. **Categories** — set Primary: "House cleaning service". Add Secondary: "Cleaning service", "Commercial cleaning service", "Carpet cleaning service" (only if offered), "Window cleaning service" (only if offered).
3. **Service area mode** — set service-area business (no storefront), and add the 10 cities from spec §2 as the served areas.
4. **Services** — add each of the 5 services with starting prices: Standard ($100+), Deep ($250+), Move-In ($250+), Move-Out ($250+), Recurring (note discounts: Weekly 20%, Bi-weekly 15%, Monthly 10%).
5. **Photos** — minimum 10 owner-real photos: 3 of clean homes (kitchen, bath, living), 3 of crew working (with consent), 2 logo treatments, 2 supply/equipment shots. Geotagged to Atlanta if EXIF allows.
6. **Hours** — Mon–Fri 7am–6pm, Sat–Sun closed.
7. **Description** — 750-char paragraph emphasizing background-checked, insured, and the 24-hour re-clean guarantee. Mention 4–5 of the 10 cities.
8. **Q&A seeding** — owner posts 6–8 questions that real customers ask (and the answers), so the Q&A panel isn't blank: pricing, supplies, pet safety, recurring discounts, areas, cancellation, insurance, getting a quote.
9. **Posts** — set a recurring task to publish a GBP Post weekly: tip ("3 things that ruin a kitchen clean"), special ("$25 off your first deep clean"), or city spotlight.
10. **Booking link** in GBP → point to `https://redskycleaning.com/quote`.

## Week 1–12: Review acquisition

Goal: **30 → 50 verified Google reviews within 90 days** (currently 14). The on-site rebuild already includes the Trustindex feed; this is about generating new reviews.

### Templates (copy/paste, customize the bracketed)

#### Template 1 — Day-of follow-up SMS (auto-send 2 hrs after job completion)

> Hi [first name], it's Red Sky! Your home is sparkling — thank you for trusting us. If we earned 5 stars today, would you share them on Google? It takes 30 seconds and means everything to a small Atlanta business. [google review link]

#### Template 2 — Day-of follow-up email (sent same day)

> Subject: How'd we do, [first name]?
>
> Hi [first name],
>
> Thank you for letting Red Sky into your home today. We hope every room exceeded your expectations.
>
> If our crew earned a 5-star rating, a quick review on Google would help us reach more Atlanta neighbors who need a trustworthy cleaner. It takes about 30 seconds:
>
> → [google review link]
>
> If anything fell short, just reply to this email — we'll come back within 24 hours and make it right at no extra cost.
>
> — The Red Sky Cleaning team

#### Template 3 — 7-day follow-up (only if no review yet)

> Subject: Quick favor?
>
> Hi [first name],
>
> A week ago we cleaned your home in [city]. We never want to be pushy, but reviews are how a small local team like ours gets seen by other Atlanta families.
>
> If you'd be open to leaving one, here's the link: [google review link]. Even 1 sentence helps.
>
> Either way, we appreciate having you as a customer.

#### Template 4 — Recurring-customer 90-day check-in

> Subject: Year in review (already?)
>
> Hi [first name],
>
> You've been a Red Sky customer for [X] months — thank you. If [name of cleaner] has been doing right by your home, would you take 30 seconds to share that on Google? It's the single biggest help you can give us. [google review link]

#### Template 5 — Anniversary text

> [first name], it's been a year since your first clean with Red Sky! 🎉 Just wanted to say thanks — and if you've ever thought about leaving a review, today would be a great day. [google review link]

### Operational notes

- **Never** offer a discount in exchange for a review — Google's policy + FTC guidance forbid it.
- **Do** mention "5 stars" because customers who didn't have a 5-star experience self-select out.
- Send Template 1 + 2 only after job completion is confirmed by the cleaner.
- Stop the sequence the moment a review posts (use the Trustindex webhook or a manual check).

## Week 4+: Blog content (8 starter topics for Atlanta cleaning queries)

The rebuild scaffolds `/blog/` but spec §19 explicitly says "Do not write blog content." These 8 titles are the owner's opportunity to outsource to a freelancer or write themselves — each is a real keyword cluster with low to moderate competition in metro Atlanta:

1. **"How Often Should You Deep-Clean a Home in Humid Atlanta Summers?"** (target: `deep clean atlanta`, `summer cleaning atlanta`)
2. **"Move-Out Cleaning Checklist: What Atlanta Landlords Actually Look For"** (target: `move out cleaning atlanta`, `atlanta apartment move out clean`)
3. **"5 Cleaning Mistakes That Damage Hardwood Floors (Common in Buckhead Homes)"** (target: `hardwood floor cleaning atlanta`)
4. **"Pet-Safe Cleaning Products We Use — and Why They Matter for Johns Creek Families"** (target: `pet safe cleaning atlanta`, `eco friendly cleaning johns creek`)
5. **"How Much Does House Cleaning Cost in Alpharetta? (Real Pricing, No 'Call for Quote')"** (target: `house cleaning prices alpharetta`)
6. **"What 14 Five-Star Atlanta Customers Say About Recurring vs One-Time Cleans"** (target: `recurring cleaning atlanta`, social-proof angle)
7. **"Move-In Cleaning in Marietta: Why You Should Schedule Before the Movers Arrive"** (target: `move in cleaning marietta`)
8. **"Are Cleaning Services in Lawrenceville Worth It? A Cost vs Time Breakdown"** (target: `cleaning service lawrenceville cost`)

Each post should be 1,000–1,500 words, have one custom hero image, link to at least 2 service pages, and link to the matching area page when applicable. Deploy posts at one per week to maintain Google's "freshness" signal.

## Tools / spend recommendation

- **GBP Insights** (free, in GBP dashboard) — track call clicks, direction requests, photo views weekly
- **Google Search Console** (free) — submit sitemap, track rankings + impressions
- **Bing Webmaster Tools** (free) — submit sitemap
- **Whitespark Local Citation Finder** ($30/mo, optional) — finds new citation opportunities
- **BrightLocal** ($35/mo, optional) — automates rank tracking across the 10 cities
- **Trustindex** (already integrated; pricing on their plan) — keep for embedded reviews
- **Total recommended monthly spend:** $0–$65 depending on which optional tools the owner adopts

## 30-60-90 day milestones

- **Day 30:** GBP fully optimized, 15 citations live, 5 new reviews collected, 2 blog posts published.
- **Day 60:** 10 new reviews total (24 cumulative), Search Console showing impressions for 50+ "[service] [city]" queries, 4 blog posts published.
- **Day 90:** 15+ new reviews (29+ cumulative), top-10 ranking on Google Maps for at least 5 of the 10 cities, 8 blog posts published. **Top-3 in 1–2 of the 10 cities is realistic; top-3 in all 10 cities is a 6–9 month horizon, not 90 days.** Spec §0 said "set the foundation now" — that foundation is what the on-site rebuild + this 90-day playbook delivers.
